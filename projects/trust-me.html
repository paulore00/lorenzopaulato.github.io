Trust
Me

A Speculative Design

Type Personal Project
Medium ESP32-CAM + 3D Print
Year 2025

Topic

On an AI that knows us better than we know ourselves — what happens when we begin to delegate even our social choices? Trust Me is a wearable facial recognition device that analyzes people around you and suggests, in real time, whether to trust them. A provocation: a physical object that makes visible the invisible algorithms already shaping our social world.

What if you let a machine
decide who to trust?

Components

NOTICE
The facial recognition system operates within predefined parameters. Performance may vary depending on lighting, positioning, and data interpretation. No guarantee of accuracy or consistency is provided. Decisions based on the system's output remain the sole responsibility of the user.

Output

The OLED display shows a trust percentage alongside a label: BAD MATCH, GOOD MATCH, PERFECT MATCH. The device then plays a brief audio message. The unsettling simplicity of this output is the point — this is how opaque algorithmic judgment feels.

Design that asks
uncomfortable questions.
